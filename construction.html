How to Build the smfBox


    The construction can be divided into three sections:

  • The box
  • Optics
  • Wiring

The Box


The box itself is composed of machined anodized aluminium parts.
The technical drawings for the aluminium components can be found on our github Here

Optics


Assembly of the scope optics occurrs in three modular stages:

  • 1. Cube attached to the table
  • 2. Excitation pathway erected and aligned to the centre and flat on Di1. The centre can be found by placing a mirror behind Di1 to reflect the bleed-through of the dichroic. This creates a cavity that the beam bounces between whilst bleeding through each time. If the reflection of the beam hitting the dichroics is aligned with the beam hitting the dichroic then the beam must be flat and centred.
  • 3. The Di1 is aligned to send the beam perpendicular to the plane of the optical table (centred on the objective’s aperture).This is checked by extending 10” rods up from the Di1 mount and mounting two frosted glass apertures on the rods at different heights.
  • 4. Confocal lenses and M3 are aligned to be flat and give a collimated beam.
  • 5. Pinhole is coarsely aligned using a power meter (looking for peak power). The pinhole should be removed again before building the rest of the emission pathway.
  • 6. Erection of the emission pathway and coarse alignment of the APDs.
  • 7. Pinhole insertion and fine alignment using the APDs, via LabView alignment tool (looking for peak photon count)
